IBM AI BUILDERS CHALLENGE WITH IBM BOB - RESEARCH BRIEF
Verified: July 13, 2026

IDENTIFICATION
--------------
This repository appears to target the AI Builders Challenge with IBM Bob, sponsored by
IBM SkillsBuild and organized by BeMyApp. It is a virtual student hackathon running from
July 1 through August 31, 2026, with separate July and August competitions.

Important clarification: this particular competition launched in 2026, so it has no
"previous years" winners. The historical winners later in this file are from Call for Code,
a separate long-running global challenge founded with IBM. They are included as relevant
examples of the kinds of focused, practical projects that have won IBM-supported events.


CURRENT JULY CHALLENGE
----------------------
Title: Reimagine Creative Industries with AI
Deadline: July 31, 2026 at 11:59 PM Eastern Time

Official prompt, short excerpt:
"Build solutions that help creators work smarter, explore new forms of expression, and
unlock new creative possibilities."

Actionable restatement:
Create an AI-powered product that expands or improves how people make content, tell
stories, design experiences, develop ideas, or participate in creative work. The strongest
interpretation treats AI as a creative collaborator while keeping meaningful human control.

Official example directions:
- AI creative partners
- Storytelling and content-creation tools
- Multimedia or multimodal experiences
- Creative ideation platforms
- Interactive media
- Personalized creative assistants

Questions the organizers want teams to consider:
- How can AI enhance creativity?
- How can AI help people create faster?
- How can AI enable genuinely new creative experiences?


WILDCARD CHALLENGE ("WILD CARE" CORRECTION)
--------------------------------------------
Title: Build Intelligent Systems for the Future of Work
Availability: A team may choose this instead of the monthly theme in July or August.

Actionable prompt restatement:
Build an intelligent system that acts as a useful collaborator for people or teams. It
should improve planning, coordination, decisions, execution, or outcomes through responsible
automation and decision support. A team submits one solution for the month: either the
monthly challenge or the Wildcard Challenge, not both.

Official example directions:
- Workflow automation
- AI co-workers
- Project-planning assistants
- Decision-intelligence platforms
- Operations and productivity systems
- Business-process orchestration

Questions the organizers want teams to consider:
- How can AI reduce repetitive work?
- How can AI improve decision-making?
- How can AI help teams achieve outcomes faster?


ENTRY AND SUBMISSION CHECKLIST
------------------------------
- Eligible entrants are higher-education students age 18 or older, subject to the official
  eligibility policy and country restrictions.
- Team size is 1-5; solo entries are allowed.
- Use IBM Bob as the required primary development tool and core project component.
- Other IBM AI technologies may supplement IBM Bob, including watsonx, Granite, LangFlow,
  and Docling.
- Complete the required IBM SkillsBuild learning activity.
- Deliver a functioning prototype or proof of concept.
- Publish a public GitHub repository.
- Include a clear README covering the problem, solution, selected theme, AI/technical
  approach and architecture, and how IBM Bob was used.
- Submit project and team details, the GitHub link, and a public demo video no longer than
  three minutes through the challenge platform.
- Verify the live rules before submission; official terms can change.


JUDGING AND PRIZES
------------------
Published judging areas:
- Technical execution
- Innovation
- Challenge fit
- Implementation and feasibility
- Real-world impact (listed on the event page)

Each monthly challenge advertises:
- 1st Place: USD 2,250
- Runner Up: USD 1,250
- Most Innovative: USD 750
- Best Use of Technology: USD 750

One project across both monthly challenges receives the USD 5,000 Grand Prize.
Total advertised prize pool: USD 15,000.

Note: the event page and official rules use slightly different labels for the USD 750
technology and innovation awards. Follow the latest official rules if wording matters.


IBM-SUPPORTED PRECEDENTS: CALL FOR CODE GRAND-PRIZE WINNERS
-----------------------------------------------------------
These are NOT past winners of the 2026 AI Builders Challenge. They are grand-prize or
top-category winners from the separate Call for Code Global Challenge.

2018 - Project OWL
Created a deployable mesh communications network for disaster zones where normal internet
service is unavailable.

2019 - Prometeo
Built a wearable safety system that monitors firefighters' exposure to hazards such as
carbon monoxide, smoke, heat, and humidity in real time.

2020 - Agrolly
Created a climate and crop-information application that helps small farmers make better
planting and business decisions.

2021 - Saaf Water
Combined hardware and software to monitor water-quality parameters and help communities
judge whether local water is safe and usable.

2022 - GardenMate
Connected home gardeners' excess produce with people who need food and offered sustainable
gardening guidance using IBM Watson.

2023 - AGNO (Developer category)
Built FARMISTAR with watsonx.ai and IBM watsonx Assistant to provide small farmers with
weather information and AI-supported crop-management strategies.

2023 also used separate top categories:
- Phyto won the University category with a geospatial and weather-analytics tool for
  rehabilitating contaminated soil.
- Synergy Squad won the ISV/Startup category with Offshelf, a food-expiry notification
  solution designed to reduce household food waste.

2024 - GoBang
Built a watsonx.ai-powered educator dashboard for customizable lesson planning, worksheets,
and recommendations, focusing on under-resourced schools in Taiwan.

2025 - DuniAfrika
Built Linda, a bilingual WhatsApp safety assistant that analyzes job-site photos and gives
practical hazard and protective-equipment guidance to informal-sector workers in Kenya.


PATTERNS WORTH LEARNING FROM
----------------------------
- Start with a specific user and an urgent, observable problem.
- Make the AI produce an actionable outcome, not merely a generic chat response.
- Use an interface already natural to the target user when possible.
- Demonstrate a complete journey from input to decision or result.
- Explain why AI is necessary and where human judgment remains in control.
- Make feasibility, accessibility, and impact visible in the prototype and demo.

RESEARCH CAUTION
----------------
The challenge website is the best source for the current prompts and submission list, while
the official rules control eligibility, deadlines, and legal requirements. One line in the
rules appears to say the README should explain relevance "in the context of racing," which
does not match either published theme and is likely a template error. Do not build around
that phrase unless the organizer confirms it.
